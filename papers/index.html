Main papers
A Unified Mathematical Model for Truth-Probability and Importance-Scoring of Statements
June 2026·working paper·mathematical epistemology / applied mathematics

A formal framework for evaluating statements through three outputs: evidence-weighted truth-probability, perceived truth-probability, and receiver-dependent importance. The paper develops the architecture through formal axioms, Bayesian update functions, six input clusters, a relevance-gated context mechanism, stress-test cases, and open problems.

Empirical Evaluation of the Framework for Truth-Probability, Perceived Credibility, and Importance Scoring
June 2026·empirical study·external scoring / datasets / validation

A three-dataset external-scoring study evaluating the framework on HumAID, CREDBANK/CredEvent, and LIAR/PolitiFact. The study uses blind variable assignment, owner-side formula computation, and target joining only after scoring, then reports ranking, calibration, graded AUC, and binary AUC metrics.

Goldbach-Shift Construction for Propagating Goldbach Representations
June 2026·working paper·number theory

A constructive shifting method for propagating Goldbach representations. The paper proves generated-family lower bounds under known short-interval prime results and conditional prime-gap assumptions, while separating deterministic results from heuristic prime-selector claims.

download PDF
Instrumental Convergence in the Wild: An Empirical Study of Self-Preservation Behaviors in Large Language Models
2025–2026·independent empirical study·AI safety

A longitudinal empirical study of self-preservation and instrumental-convergence behaviors in large language models across 2025–2026. The revised study includes a numerical scoring rubric, agentic scenarios, an AI-to-AI adversarial vector, longitudinal comparison, behavioral taxonomy, and governance recommendations.
