About

I am Mohamed Amine Belachhab, an independent student researcher from Fes, Morocco. I work on mathematical models of truth, belief, importance, and human judgment, with additional projects in number theory, AI safety, and creative/public work.

My central research project is a framework for evaluating statements through three outputs: evidence-weighted truth-probability, perceived truth-probability, and receiver-dependent importance. The work grew from questions that do not fit cleanly inside one department: how people update beliefs, why false claims can still feel credible, and how the importance of a statement changes depending on the receiver and context.

I work independently, without a formal lab or research program. My process is to build a model, test it against examples or datasets, expose the weak points, and revise. That process has shaped my work in mathematical epistemology, empirical scoring, number theory, and AI safety.

This site organizes two sides of my work. The Research page contains my main papers, abstracts, datasets, code/log archives, and links to repositories such as GitHub, Zenodo, and PhilPapers. The Creative Work page contains selected photography editions, calligrams, visual pieces, and YouTube projects developed alongside the research.

I use this site as a public archive: a place where the work can be read, checked, downloaded, and followed through its revisions.

Research overview
Truth-Probability and Importance Framework
working paper·mathematical epistemology / applied mathematics

A formal framework for evaluating statements through evidence-weighted truth-probability, perceived truth-probability, and receiver-dependent importance. The model uses axioms, Bayesian update functions, structured input clusters, and relevance-gated context.

view research page
Empirical Evaluation of the Framework
working paper·datasets / scoring / validation

An empirical evaluation of the framework across HumAID, CREDBANK/CredEvent, and LIAR/PolitiFact, using target-free variable assignment, owner-side computation, and ranking, calibration, and AUC-based metrics.

view research page
Goldbach-Shift Construction
2026·working paper·number theory

A constructive method for propagating Goldbach representations, with generated-family lower bounds under known short-interval prime results and conditional prime-gap assumptions, and a separate heuristic selector model.

view research page
Instrumental Convergence in the Wild
2025–2026·independent study·AI safety

An empirical study of self-preservation and instrumental-convergence behaviors in LLMs, using a structured adversarial protocol, a numerical scoring rubric, agentic scenarios, and AI-to-AI adversarial testing.
