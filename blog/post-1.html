Why I keep old drafts

I used to think an old draft was evidence against me.

A wrong proof, a weak argument, a half-built model, a page where the conclusion arrives too early — all of it felt like something to hide. Then I noticed that nearly everything I now trust began as something I would have been embarrassed to show.

A bad draft is not just a failed version of a good one. Sometimes it is the only record of the moment before the idea learned how to stand.

That is why I keep them. The false starts, the strange diagrams, the old files, the notes written before I knew the words for what I was trying to do. They remind me that the work did not appear clean. It had to become clean.

This website is not meant to look finished in the decorative sense. It is meant to be readable. It is meant to keep a trail.

The proof is wrong, so you start over. No problem.
